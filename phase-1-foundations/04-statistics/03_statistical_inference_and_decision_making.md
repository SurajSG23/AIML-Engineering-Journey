# Statistical Inference and Decision Making

> Statistics is not just about describing data.
>
> It is about making decisions from limited information.

---

# The Core Problem

Suppose:

1000 people were surveyed.

Can we draw conclusions about 1 million people?

Statistical inference helps us answer this.

---

# Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

One of the most important concepts in statistics.

The theorem states:

If we repeatedly take samples and calculate their averages, those averages tend to form a normal distribution.

Even if original data is not normal.

---

Why it matters:

Most statistical methods rely on this property.

---

# Sampling Distribution

Distribution of a statistic across many samples.

Example:

Take 1000 samples.

Calculate average for each.

Plot those averages.

Result:

Sampling distribution.

---

# Standard Error

Measures uncertainty in a sample estimate.

Small standard error:

More confidence.

Large standard error:

Less confidence.

---

# Confidence Intervals

A range likely to contain the true value.

Example:

Average salary:

₹50,000 ± ₹3,000

Confidence Interval:

₹47,000 to ₹53,000

---

Interpretation:

We are reasonably confident the true average lies in that range.

---

# Margin of Error

Distance around an estimate.

Example:

50% ± 2%

Margin of error = 2%

---

# Hypothesis Testing

Used to determine whether evidence supports a claim.

---

# Null Hypothesis (H0)

Default assumption.

Example:

New medicine does not work better.

---

# Alternative Hypothesis (H1)

Claim being tested.

Example:

New medicine works better.

---

# P-Value

Measures strength of evidence against H0.

Small p-value:

Strong evidence against null hypothesis.

---

Common threshold:

0.05

---

If:

p < 0.05

Reject H0

---

If:

p ≥ 0.05

Fail to reject H0

---

# Type I Error

False Positive.

You think improvement exists.

Reality:

No improvement exists.

---

# Type II Error

False Negative.

Improvement exists.

You fail to detect it.

---

# A/B Testing

Widely used in industry.

---

Example

Version A:

Old website

Version B:

New website

Question:

Does B increase conversions?

Run experiment.

Collect data.

Apply hypothesis testing.

Make decision.

# Key Takeaways

1. Statistical inference makes conclusions from samples.
2. CLT is one of the most important theorems.
